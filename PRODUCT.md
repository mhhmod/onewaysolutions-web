# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

One Way Solutions serves B2B buyers, engineers, contractors, facility operators, procurement teams, and project owners looking for electrical, solar, safety, fire, telecom, and industrial supply solutions in Egypt and the wider region. Visitors arrive to understand capabilities, inspect catalog detail, collect product options, and request a quote without creating an account.

Admins are internal One Way Solutions staff who manage catalog categories, product imagery, project references, and quote requests from a separate private admin subdomain.

## Product Purpose

The public website presents One Way Solutions as a credible industrial solutions supplier and manufacturer. It must make the catalog easy to browse, expose all available product imagery, and convert interested visitors into quote requests.

The primary public workflow is: land on the site, browse product categories, inspect products and images, add items to a quote request, then submit contact details once at the request step. The website does not show prices, does not collect payments, and does not require customer login.

The admin workflow is private and task-focused: manage products, categories, product images, project content, site settings, and quote request status.

## Brand Personality

Precise, grounded, capable.

The brand should feel technical and trustworthy without becoming cold. It should communicate industrial competence, procurement clarity, and practical engineering support. The tone is direct, professional, and helpful.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS landing pages, decorative glassmorphism, stock-office imagery, gradient-heavy AI templates, consumer marketplace patterns, playful e-commerce language, visible prices, account-first flows, and over-designed card grids that make industrial products feel lightweight.

Avoid hiding catalog detail behind forms. Customers should be able to see the catalog first and provide their details only when requesting a quote.

## Design Principles

1. Show the real catalog first. Product imagery and categories are the strongest proof of capability.
2. Make quoting simple. Browsing should be open, and the only form should appear at quote submission.
3. Separate public trust from private operations. The public domain sells confidence; the admin subdomain manages work.
4. Use industrial clarity. Layouts should support scanning, comparison, and technical inspection.
5. Build English-first, Arabic-ready. The interface starts in English but must not assume LTR-only structure.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for public and admin surfaces. Use visible labels, keyboard-accessible controls, descriptive image alt text, clear focus states, adequate color contrast, reduced-motion support, and mobile layouts without horizontal overflow. Arabic expansion and RTL layout must be accounted for in spacing, navigation, product grids, and quote forms.
